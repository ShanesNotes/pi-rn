# Nephrology consult — 11:30

Consulted for AKI on CKD stage 3b and metformin restart criteria. The trigger remains the K 5.6 and creatinine 2.2 from baseline 1.4 after home losartan, spironolactone, and ibuprofen exposure recorded on admission [evt-004-0017]. Recovery is on track: K 4.6 and Cr 1.8 on AM BMP after volume removal of approximately 920 mL [evt-004-0035, evt-004-0037].

Assessment. Pre-renal AKI on CKD precipitated by RAAS, MRA, and NSAID exposure during volume overload. eGFR climbing from 30 toward baseline. No indication for renal replacement therapy.

Recommendations.

- Continue medication holds for losartan, spironolactone, and NSAIDs. Decision on RAAS/MRA reintroduction belongs with outpatient cardiology and nephrology together, not in-house.
- Hold metformin until creatinine is at or below 1.5 on a confirmatory BMP, ideally at the outpatient nephrology visit. Restart at 500 mg PO daily then re-titrate.
- Avoid NSAIDs indefinitely.
- Repeat BMP daily while inpatient; confirm at outpatient nephrology visit in 2 weeks with home BP/weight log.

Sign off after discharge BMP if trend continues.
